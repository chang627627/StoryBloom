01 · The problem

The same story, every night.

Bedtime is repetitive by design; children find comfort in the familiar. But the story itself rarely catches what the child actually carried into bed. The day's small frustrations, the new feeling without a name, the question never asked. Parents reach for the same five books, or skip the story entirely.

Generic AI storytellers don't fix this. A chat box at bedtime is the wrong instrument. A pre-reader can't type. A tired parent doesn't want a prompt window. The product that meets this moment has to be quieter than that.

02 · The insight

Children don't process
the day in words.

A three-year-old can't say "I felt overwhelmed at preschool." But she can point to a color. She can say it felt like rain. She can pick the shape of the feeling out of three soft drawings. She has a small companion in mind, even if she can't name why.

Color A color for the day
Weather The weather inside
Shape The shape of the feeling
Friend A little something for company

Four nonverbal picks. The day is now legible to a system, without ever asking the child to put a feeling into language. From those four tiny acts, the night's story is built.

05 · Design language

Bedtime-warm,
quietly geometric.

Palette

Lav
Sky
Butter
Peach
Mint
Coral
Peri

Six pastels for the day's color picks; periwinkle for every CTA. Coral is activating; periwinkle is sleepy. Bedtime gets the sleepy one.

Type

Aa Fraunces · Stories
Aa Nunito · Interface

Fraunces holds the storytelling: display optical size, italic for the warmth. Nunito for every label, button, and step. One narrative voice, one interface voice.

Worlds

Moon Garden
Sleepy Forest
Cloud Sea
Star Village
Dream Hill

Five hand-drawn worlds, each its own time of day. No shared motifs; every bedtime looks different.

Surface & rhythm

Rounded cards, 20 to 28px radii. Soft pastel blobs behind glass, 24px blur. Animations stay slow: 5 to 8s for floats, 6s for the breathing orb, ~850ms for the writing pulse. Nothing demands attention. Everything earns it.

06 · Principles

Quiet, on purpose.

Four design choices the product is built from. Each one closes a door other AI apps leave open.

i.

Invisible AI.

No chat, no model picker, no "regenerate." A 0.85-second writing pulse and the line "written for tonight" prove the story is fresh. The intelligence is the medium, never the interface.

ii.

Nonverbal first.

A pre-reader can't type, but she can point. Every required input is a tap on a colored chip, a soft icon, a face. Words are an output, not an input.

iii.

One of each.

One color, one weather, one shape, one friend, one world, one story. Not options to compare; picks to commit to. Choice fatigue is the enemy of bedtime.

iv.

Bedtime, not engagement.

The last screen is Sleep Mode: a dim twilight with no CTA, no streak, no "tomorrow's story." Bedtime apps that fight for attention at bedtime have already lost the brief.
